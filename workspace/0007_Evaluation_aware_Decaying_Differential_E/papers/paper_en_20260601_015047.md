# Abstract
This paper proposes a novel evolutionary optimization method, Evaluation-aware Decaying Differential Evolution (EA-DDE), to jointly optimize social welfare and fairness (e.g., Jain index, minimum-beneficiary guarantee) in the price-setting problem of Local Energy Communities (LECs). EA-DDE (1) controls DE mutation and crossover intensities with a generation-wise decay schedule that induces an exploration→exploitation transition, and (2) streamlines costly, stochastic simulation evaluations by combining evaluation-aware racing (progressive sampling: cheap→expanded→full) repeated within each generation, surrogate-guided budget allocation, and random audits. Fairness constraints are handled via a Lagrangian formulation with primal–dual updates to adjust λ. We provide pseudocode, statistical sampling specifications for error/false-negative control, complexity analysis, and a detailed reproducibility plan, and we describe an experimental design (scenarios, baselines, comparisons, evaluation metrics) to validate the proposed method.

# 1. Introduction
Local Energy Communities (LECs) perform local energy trading and price-setting using distributed generation (e.g., PV) and flexible demand management. Pricing policies must maximize overall social welfare while ensuring fairness so that particular groups or vulnerable agents do not suffer excessive disadvantage. In practice, evaluations of candidate pricing policies are costly and noisy due to renewable variability, demand uncertainty, and simulation-based cost assessments. Therefore, there is a practical challenge of efficiently finding high-quality solutions (in terms of social welfare and fairness) under a limited evaluation budget.

Contributions
- EA-DDE: We propose the first integrated algorithm that combines a generation-wise decay schedule and intra-generation evaluation-aware racing (cheap→expanded→full evaluations) within the DE framework. This enables efficient allocation of evaluation budget and explicit control of the exploration→exploitation transition in costly, stochastic evaluation environments.
- Lagrangian handling of fairness constraints: We treat social welfare as the objective and fairness indicators g(x) as constraints, and manage the trade-off online by adjusting the Lagrange multiplier λ_t via primal–dual updates.
- Theoretical and practical safety mechanisms: We present Hoeffding/Chernoff-based confidence-interval specifications for early elimination with controlled false-negative probability, a random audit probability p_audit, and a surrogate-aware budget allocation rule that accounts for surrogate uncertainty.
- Reproducibility: We provide pseudocode, hyperparameters, complexity analysis, and a detailed experimental protocol (datasets, scenarios, evaluation metrics, statistical tests).

Organization: Related work (Section 2), Methodology (Section 3), Experimental design (Section 4), Conclusion (Section 5).

# 2. Related Work
- Differential Evolution (1995–1997) and adaptive variants: DE is widely used for continuous optimization. Adaptive variants such as jDE and SaDE adjust F and CR adaptively. Our method is DE-based but explicitly controls the exploration→exploitation transition via a decay schedule.
- Multi-objective / Fairness-aware optimization: There is literature treating fairness as a multi-objective criterion or as a constraint. We treat fairness as a constraint and combine it with an online Lagrangian update.
- Sampling and racing in costly-evaluation environments: Methods such as F-Race, successive halving, and Hyperband progressively increase the evaluation budget for promising candidates to save resources. This work integrates such racing mechanisms inside DE at the generation level and leverages surrogate predictions and their uncertainties to allocate budget.
- Surrogate-assisted evolutionary optimization: Many works combine surrogate models (GPs, RFs, etc.) with evolutionary algorithms for expensive evaluations. EA-DDE proposes a budget allocation rule that explicitly considers surrogate predictive uncertainty (e.g., predictive variance).
- Statistical safety: We borrow theoretical foundations for early stopping and false-negative control using Hoeffding/Chernoff inequalities.

Distinguishing contributions: (1) Integrating intra-generation evaluation scheduling within DE together with mutation parameter decay, (2) Lagrangian online control of fairness constraints, (3) combining practical safety mechanisms including statistical false-negative control.

# 3. Method

Problem statement
- Decision variable x ∈ X ⊂ R^d: pricing-policy parameters for the local community (e.g., time-of-use rates, retirement-subsidy parameters).
- Objective: maximize f(x) := E_ω[W(x; ω)] (expected social welfare; ω denotes uncertainty samples).
- Constraints: g_i(x) ≤ c_i (i = 1..m). For example, fairness(x) ≥ φ_0 can be expressed as g(x) = φ_0 - fairness(x) ≤ 0.
- Evaluations are costly and stochastic: a full evaluation uses N_sims simulation runs.

Algorithm overview
- Base: DE generational loop (t = 0..T-1) with population size NP.
- Each generation contains K stages of evaluation-aware racing: at stage k each candidate is evaluated with n_k samples (cheap: small → expanded → full: large) to compute an estimate μ̂_k and confidence interval CI_k. At each round, statistically poor candidates are eliminated early.
- Surrogate: A surrogate model (e.g., Gaussian Process or Random Forest) is trained/updated using current candidate statistics (μ̂_k, s^2_k) to produce predicted mean μ̃ and predictive uncertainty σ̃. Candidates with high predictive uncertainty receive additional evaluation budget.
- Lagrangian handling: The Lagrangian objective is L(x, λ) = -μ̃(x) + λ * max{0, ĝ(x)} (sign chosen considering maximization). λ update:
  λ_{t+1} = max(0, λ_t + η_t * (ĝ_pop^+ - ρ)),
  where ĝ_pop^+ is the estimated constraint violation for selected candidates (or population average), ρ is an allowance (bias correction), and η_t is the step size (possibly decaying).
- Exploration→exploitation decay: mutation factor F_t, crossover CR_t, and mutation probability p_mut(t) are adjusted by decay schedules:
  F_t = F_min + (F_0 - F_min) * exp(-α * t / T)
  CR_t = CR_max - (CR_max - CR_min) * exp(-β * t / T)
  where α, β ≥ 0 control decay speed. Large F and varied CR early for exploration; smaller values later to promote convergence.
- Audits: A fraction p_audit of candidates that are eliminated early are randomly selected for full evaluation to reduce false negatives.

Pseudocode (summary)
Inputs: NP, T, K, n_1 < ... < n_K (sample sizes per stage), total evaluation budget B, F_0, F_min, CR_min, CR_max, α, β, η_0, p_audit, δ (confidence level)
Initialize: population P_0 (size NP), λ_0 = 0, surrogate S (empty)
for t = 0..T-1:
  compute F_t, CR_t by decay schedule
  generate offspring Q via DE mutation/crossover (F_t, CR_t)
  C = P_t ∪ Q
  for k = 1..K:
    for each candidate x in C not yet eliminated:
      run additional n_k - n_{k-1} simulation samples (n_0 := 0) → update sample mean μ̂_k(x), variance s^2_k(x)
      compute CI_k(x) (Hoeffding/Chernoff-based or t-interval)
    eliminate candidate x if CI_k upper bound < top candidate's CI_k lower bound minus margin (statistical criterion, δ allocation)
    update surrogate S using current sample data (μ̂_k etc.)
    allocate additional budget priority: candidates with high μ̃ + high σ̃ or with wide current CI receive priority
  perform random audits: for eliminated candidates, with probability p_audit perform full evaluation to detect false negatives; if audit results are significant, restore candidate
  select next generation P_{t+1} from surviving candidates using Lagrangian score:
    score(x) = μ̃(x) - λ_t * max{0, ĝ(x)}
  update λ_{t+1} = max(0, λ_t + η_t * (ĝ_sel^+ - ρ))
endfor
Output: final candidate set (e.g., Pareto front candidates)

Statistical early-elimination rules and false-negative control
- At stage k, the sample mean μ̂_k(x) for candidate x is assumed to be the mean of independent samples (or using properly separated cross-validation samples).
- From Hoeffding's inequality, for n samples:
  P(|μ̂ - μ| ≥ ε) ≤ 2 exp(-2 n ε^2 / (b - a)^2),
  where [a, b] is the performance range. For a target false-negative tolerance δ', the required n satisfies
  n ≥ ((b - a)^2 / (2 ε^2)) ln(2/δ').
- Early elimination criterion: To eliminate candidate x in favor of y at stage k, require
  μ̂_k(x) + CI_upper(x) < μ̂_k(y) - CI_lower(y) - Δ_margin,
  where Δ_margin is a safety margin. Use the union bound across stages and comparisons to control the overall δ_total = Σ_{stages,comparisons} δ_k.
- Audit probability p_audit: The ideal number of audits A to achieve false-negative probability δ_FN can be approximated by
  A = -ln(δ_FN) / ln(1/(1 - r)),
  where r is the approximate proportion of eliminated candidates. In practice, a small p_audit can empirically reduce false negatives.

Lagrangian λ update (concrete rule)
- λ_{t+1} = max(0, λ_t + η_t * (ḡ_t^+ - ρ))
  where ḡ_t^+ = mean_{x ∈ P_{t+1}} max(0, ĝ(x)) or the worst candidate's ĝ
  and η_t = η_0 / sqrt(t + 1) (recommended decaying step size).
- Interpretation: Large constraint violation increases λ and thus reinforces constraint enforcement; small violation allows λ to decrease.

Using surrogate predictions and uncertainty
- The surrogate is trained on pairs (x, μ̂, s^2). Examples: GP for low-dimensional problems or RF for higher-dimensional cases.
- Budget allocation priority score:
  score_eval(x) = μ̃(x) + κ * σ̃(x) + γ * CI_width(x),
  with κ, γ ≥ 0. Candidates with high uncertainty or wide CI receive more samples.

Complexity and scalability
- Worst-case per-generation evaluation cost: O(NP * n_K) simulations. In practice racing induces early elimination so average cost << NP * n_K.
- Surrogate training cost: GP O(N^3) for N data points; RF O(N log N). Practically, RF or sparse GP is recommended.
- Parallelism: Candidate simulations are independent and amenable to parallel/distributed execution. Increasing surrogate update intervals can reduce communication overhead.

Practical hyperparameters (recommended initial values)
- NP = 50, T = 100, K = 3, n = [5, 20, 100] (cheap→full), B ≈ NP * T * avg(n_k) as a budget constraint
- F_0 = 0.8, F_min = 0.2, CR_min = 0.1, CR_max = 0.9, α = 5, β = 5
- η_0 = 0.1, p_audit = 0.05, δ_total = 0.05, κ = 1.0, γ = 0.5
- These defaults should be adjusted according to problem characteristics; sensitivity analysis is recommended.

# 4. Experiments
Objectives: Evaluate whether EA-DDE (under limited evaluation budget) (1) improves social welfare and (2) improves fairness metrics (e.g., Jain index, minimum utility), and (3) assess evaluation-efficiency and false-negative control.

Datasets and scenarios
- Synthetic scenarios: Energy community simulator with varying sizes (small: 20 households, medium: 100 households, large: 500 households), PV penetration rates, and demand-elasticity distributions. Each agent's utility function and price response are modeled (e.g., linear demand elasticity with noise).
- Realistic / semi-real scenarios: Time-series demand reconstruction and synthesized PV production based on public consumption datasets (e.g., REFIT, UK-DALE, Pecan Street).
- Uncertainty modeling: Random sampling of PV production based on seasonal/weather variability, daily/weekly demand fluctuations, and simulation random seeds to introduce evaluation noise.

Baselines
- DE (vanilla), jDE, SaDE
- MODE: NSGA-II or a MODE variant treating social welfare and fairness as simultaneous objectives
- Surrogate-assisted DE (without intra-generation racing)
- DE combined with successive halving / Hyperband (racing applied outside generations)
- Bayesian Optimization (BO) as a standard comparator for expensive evaluations

Evaluation metrics
- Social welfare: mean total welfare (mean and distribution)
- Fairness: Jain's fairness index, minimum utility among agents
- Pareto-front approximation: hypervolume (social welfare vs fairness)
- Evaluation efficiency: performance per number of simulation runs
- False-negative rate: proportion of early-elimination errors detected by audits
- Computation time (total including surrogate training)

Experimental protocol
- 30 random seeds per setting; identical initialization and simulation samples across methods for fair comparison
- Budget levels: low, medium, high: B_low = 0.2 * baseline, B_med = baseline, B_high = 2 * baseline
- Statistical tests: Wilcoxon signed-rank test (p < 0.05) and bootstrap 95% confidence intervals for performance metrics
- Ablation studies: (A) full EA-DDE, (B) EA-DDE without racing, (C) EA-DDE without surrogate, (D) EA-DDE without decay (fixed F, CR), (E) EA-DDE without audit
- Sensitivity analyses over α, β, p_audit, η_0, K, n_k

Expected validation points
- Under limited budgets, EA-DDE is expected to achieve higher mean social welfare and improved fairness compared to baseline DE/SaDE (especially in high-noise, high-uncertainty settings).
- The combination of racing and surrogate should yield faster early convergence and better Pareto approximations for the same budget.
- The audit mechanism and confidence-interval-based elimination should control false-negative rates while enabling budget savings.

Reproducibility
- Code will be released (GitHub) including simulator and scenario scripts. Experiment settings (hyperparameters, seeds) will be documented. Versions of surrogate libraries (e.g., scikit-learn, GPy) will be specified.

# 5. Conclusion
We proposed EA-DDE for the problem of pricing policy design in Local Energy Communities under costly, stochastic evaluations. EA-DDE controls the exploration→exploitation transition in DE via a decay schedule and integrates intra-generation evaluation-aware racing, surrogate-guided budget allocation, Lagrangian fairness control, and auditing. We provide Hoeffding/Chernoff-based specifications for false-negative control, practical hyperparameter recommendations, complexity analysis, and an experimental design aimed at simultaneously improving social welfare and fairness under budget constraints. Future work includes large-scale distributed execution (edge parallelization), integration with online real-time price updates, and field deployment in real LECs.
